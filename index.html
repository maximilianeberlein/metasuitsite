  • Metasuit Sensutional Realities

    We are eight mechanical engineering students at ETH Zurich, working with the Soft Robotics Lab and Sensory-Motor Systems Lab, aiming to revolutionise Metaverse experiences by applying the novel HASEL technology in unseen and impactful ways.


    Stage 1

    Research and develop self-sensing human-like HASEL muscle prototypes.

    Stage 2

    Integrate sensor into soft textiles and read data into virtual environment.

    Stage 3

    Map movement of the entire human body precisely into VR and test applications.
  • Our Vision

    MetaSuit envisions the integration of soft artificial muscles into a full-body suit which allows to map the pose and movement of people into a VR/AR space. The project’s main objective is to create a soft, light and natural feeling suit that will allow users to move and interact within VR environments and overcome many of the drawbacks of vision based motion capture systems and handheld devices.

    • Include Actuation

      We strive for applications which incorporate force-feedback mechanisms.

    • Revolutionise VR interaction

      Medicine, Physiotherapy, Entertainment, Education, Civil Protection.

    • Cost Reduction in the Industry

      We work with cost-efficient, easily producible HASEL actuators

  • Status Quo

    Current VR peripherals are often vision-based and quite expensive. The drawbacks of these systems are that they only function properly in good lighting conditions and stop working entirely once the device is out of the visible range. Further, these systems often rely on bulky actuators or input devices, which can hinder free motion of the user. For this reason, we are currently researching knowledge about HASEL actuators, so we can later implement this technology into our suit.

    Innovation

    For MetaSuit, we will be using Hydraulically Amplified Self-healing Electrostatic (HASEL) actuators. Our team is working with several experts and researchers in the field of soft and self-sensing actuators. This combined knowledge allows us to design and create our envisioned suit, which can then be used in various other applications. The innovation we create will spark further innovation in the industry.

  • Focusproject

    The focus project is a curricular program open to students of mechanical and electrical engineering at ETH Zurich in the final year of their bachelor’s degree. During two semesters, the team commits itself to solving complex engineering problems under the supervision of a professor. The project allows students to develop technical knowledge, project management skills and to build relationships with academic as well as industrial partners.
